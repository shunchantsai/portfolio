I am an analyst working at the intersection of geopolitical competition, 
critical infrastructure resilience, and digital information operations. 
My work is grounded in physical and social science methodology — BSc 
Geography, MSc Climate Change, and MRes Urban Sustainability and 
Resilience, all at University College London — applied to systemic risk 
across political, environmental, and cognitive domains.

## Methodology

My analysis separates political noise from structural reality — tracing 
how institutional features, material incentives, and information 
environments produce systemic outcomes that are difficult to observe in 
real time and therefore systematically underpriced in conventional risk 
assessments. I apply deterrence theory, political economy frameworks, and 
spatial risk modeling to cross-domain problems spanning Taiwan's strategic 
environment, climate-linked infrastructure vulnerability, and cognitive 
warfare dynamics. I utilise iterative AI-assisted workflows for research 
synthesis while maintaining full editorial control over analytical outputs.
